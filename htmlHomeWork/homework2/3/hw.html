TAG

[tæg]

noun

  1. a label attached to someone or something for the purpose of identification or to give other information.
    1. "he gave his pet a collar with a metal name tag"
    2. synonyms: ticket, badge, mark, marker, tab, tally, sticker, docket, stub, chit, chitt, counterfoi, flag, stamp
    3. "a price tag"
  2. a small piece or part that is attached to a main body.

verb

  1. attach a label to.
    1. "mothers suspected that their babies had been wrongly tagged during an alarm at the hospital"
    2. synonyms:label, attach tags to, put a label on, mark, ticket, earmark, identify, docket, flag, indicate
    3. "the bottles were tagged with colour-coded labels"
  2. add to something, especially as an afterthought or with no real connection.
  3. "she meant to tag her question on at the end of her remarks"
  4. synonyms:add, tack, join, attach, append, affix, annex
  5. "their heads were so large that the rest of their bodies seemed tagged on as an afterthought"
